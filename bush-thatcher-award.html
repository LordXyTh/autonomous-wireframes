About the Award

Honouring Champions of Freedom

The Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom is the International Democracy Union's highest honour, awarded annually to an individual who has made an outstanding contribution to the advancement of freedom, democracy, and human rights.

Named after two of the IDU's founding figures — President George H.W. Bush and Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher — the award recognises extraordinary courage and leadership in defending democratic values at personal risk or political cost.

Recipients are nominated by IDU member parties and selected by the Executive Committee for their exceptional contribution to the cause of liberty.

🏆

Award Criteria

  • ★ Outstanding contribution to democratic freedom
  • ★ Personal courage in advancing democratic values
  • ★ Service to the cause of human rights globally
  • ★ Commitment to rule of law and accountability
  • ★ Inspiration to democratic movements worldwide

2025 Laureate

Tidjane Thiam

👤
Tidjane Thiam
2025 Laureate
Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom 2025

The IDU presented its 2025 Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom to Tidjane Thiam at a ceremony in London on December 4, 2025. Thiam received the award in recognition of his principled commitment to democratic governance in Côte d'Ivoire and his courage in the face of political persecution.

A former CEO of Credit Suisse and leader of the PDCI political party, Thiam has demonstrated extraordinary personal bravery and dedication to the democratic process, inspiring millions across West Africa.
