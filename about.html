Our Story

About the IDU

Our Mission

The International Democracy Union is a working group of centre-right political parties, which enables them to establish contacts and to co-operate on matters of policy and organisation. We provide a forum for parties committed to freedom, democracy, and human rights.

Our Vision

A world in which democracy, freedom, the rule of law, and individual human rights are protected and promoted on every continent. A world where centre-right democratic values prevail and where no individual or nation must live under tyranny or oppression.

Our History

From a vision shared by three world leaders to a global alliance spanning six continents

1983

Founded in London

The IDU was founded on June 24, 1983 in London by Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher (UK), President Ronald Reagan (USA), Chancellor Helmut Kohl (Germany), and leaders of centre-right parties from across the democratic world.

1990s

Expansion After the Cold War

Following the fall of the Berlin Wall, the IDU welcomed newly democratised parties from Central and Eastern Europe, significantly expanding its reach and membership base.

2003

Bush-Thatcher Award Established

The IDU establishes the prestigious Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom, honouring individuals who make extraordinary contributions to the advancement of democratic values worldwide.

2023

40th Anniversary

The IDU celebrates four decades of championing centre-right democratic values, with 80+ member parties now spanning 6 continents and representing hundreds of millions of voters.

Governance Structure

🏛️

Congress

The supreme governing body, meeting every two years. All full members have equal voting rights.

👥

Executive Committee

Elected by Congress to direct IDU activities between Congresses. Meets annually.

✍️

Secretary General

Leads the IDU Secretariat, manages day-to-day operations, and implements Executive Committee decisions.

Our Core Values

🗳️

Democracy

Free and fair elections, representative government, and participatory civic life.

🕊️

Freedom

Individual liberty, free markets, freedom of speech, and freedom of the press.

⚖️

Rule of Law

Independent judiciary, constitutional government, and equality before the law.

🤝

Human Rights

Universal human rights, dignity for all, and protection of minorities.
